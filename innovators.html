Inside the STEM Classroom: How Senior High Students Are Innovating for the Future

Across the Philippines, Senior High School students enrolled in the STEM Strand are pushing boundaries—from eco-innovations to energy solutions—demonstrating that real-world impact can begin in the classroom.

From Idea to Community Benefit

In San Carlos City, STEM students from Colegio de Sto. Tomas-Recoletos showcased their prototypes to City Mayor Renato Gustilo on March 11, 2024. One team presented the Viable Arduino-Based River Water Level Monitoring System (VAMos), while another introduced the Concentrated Autonomous Solar Technology (CAST)—innovations designed to support remote and flood-prone barangays.

Building Sustainable Solutions

Through the Shell NXplorers program, several student groups created sustainable technologies addressing community needs:

  • Team FarmHer Innovators from Palawan National High School developed the Veggie Crate Tracker and Mark-It App, which help markets monitor produce supply and freshness.
  • Team Maomag of Tagbilaran City Science High School engineered a solar-and-wind-powered desalination system to turn seawater into drinking water.
  • Team Portabio from Gusa Regional Science High School transformed food waste into portable energy via power banks.

Hands-on Research & Renewables

In Davao City, around 80–100 STEM students joined "Project Likas-Kaya!," an immersive two-day research activity held June 19–20, 2025. Organized by DOE–Mindanao and JCI Davao, the event focused on sustainable energy. Participants engaged with key industry players and learned practical solar PV technology applications.
